What is Diabetes Mellitus?

Type 1 v.s Type 2 Diabetes

-Diabetes Mellitus is a disease in which the body improperly interacts with blood sugar.
-Inability to properly digest sugar results in many symptoms, stemming from excess sugar in the body.
-Type 1 is considered to be hereditary, while Type 2 is commonly acquired throughout adolescence and adulthood.
-While both Type 1 and Type 2 are considered chronic diseases, many steps can be taken to reduce symptoms.

Statistics

-More than 130 million adults are living with diabetes or prediabetes in the US. (CDC, 2022)
-This accounts for nearly 12% of the U.S. Population
-Heart Disease is not only the number 1 cause of death in America, it is the number 1 cause of death in people with Type 2 diabetes.

Prevention and Awareness

-Treatment for Type 1 Diabetes mainly includes regular insulin injections.
-In extreme cases, Type 2 Diabetes is also treated with insulin supplementation, however the main mitigative practices include a healthy diet and an active lifestyle.
-November is American Diabetes Month.
- A blue ribbon is used to represent Diabetes Awareness and advocation for education on the Diabetes Epidemic.
